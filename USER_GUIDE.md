## List Builder & Dynamic Segmentation

### What is it?
Create targeted lists of candidates based on specific criteria like skills, experience, location, and work preferences. Lists can be dynamic (automatically updated) or static (fixed snapshot).

### How to use it

#### Step 1: Preview Your List
Before creating a list, preview how many candidates match your criteria.

1. Navigate to **List Builder** in your dashboard
2. Set your segmentation rules:
   - **Skills**: Select required skills (e.g., "React", "Python")
   - **Experience**: Set min/max years of experience
   - **Location**: Choose preferred cities
   - **Work Setting**: Remote, hybrid, or on-site
   - **Salary Range**: Set expected salary range
3. Click **Preview** to see estimated results
4. Review the statistics:
   - Total candidate count
   - Top skills distribution
   - Average experience level
   - Location breakdown
   - Match quality distribution

#### Step 2: Create Your List
Once you're satisfied with the preview:

1. Click **Create List**
2. Give your list a name (e.g., "Senior React Developers - Riyadh")
3. Add an optional description
4. Choose list type:
   - **Dynamic**: Automatically updates as new candidates match
   - **Static**: Fixed snapshot of current matches
5. Click **Save**

#### Step 3: Use Your List
Your list is now ready for:
- Email campaigns
- Bulk outreach
- Talent pipeline management
- Performance tracking

### Tips for Success
- Start with broader criteria and narrow down
- Use dynamic lists for ongoing recruitment
- Use static lists for specific campaigns
- Refresh dynamic lists weekly to catch new candidates
- Track list performance to optimize criteria

## Email Performance Alerts

### What is it?
Automatically monitor your email campaigns and get notified when performance drops below thresholds or deviates from industry benchmarks.

### How to use it

#### Step 1: Create an Alert
1. Go to **Performance Alerts** in your dashboard
2. Click **Create New Alert**
3. Configure your alert:
   - **Alert Name**: Descriptive name (e.g., "Low Open Rate Warning")
   - **Alert Type**: Choose from:
     - Underperformance (low metrics)
     - High Engagement (exceptional performance)
     - Low Deliverability (bounce/spam issues)
     - Benchmark Deviation (vs industry standards)
   - **Trigger Conditions**:
     - Metric to track (open rate, click rate, etc.)
     - Threshold value (e.g., below 20%)
     - Time window (e.g., last 7 days)
   - **Notification Channels**: Email and/or dashboard
   - **Recipients**: Who should be notified
4. Click **Create Alert**

#### Step 2: Monitor Alerts
- View active alerts in your dashboard
- Check alert history to see past triggers
- Review triggered alerts with context and recommendations

#### Step 3: Acknowledge and Act
When an alert triggers:
1. Review the alert details
2. Check the recommended actions
3. Implement improvements
4. Mark alert as acknowledged with notes
5. Monitor if performance improves

### Industry Benchmarks
- **Open Rate**: 25% (good), below 20% (concerning)
- **Click Rate**: 3.5% (good), below 2% (concerning)
- **Bounce Rate**: Below 2% (good), above 5% (concerning)
- **Engagement Rate**: 28% (good), below 20% (concerning)

### Common Alert Scenarios

**Low Open Rate Alert**
- **Cause**: Poor subject lines, wrong send time, list fatigue
- **Action**: A/B test subject lines, optimize send time, clean list

**High Bounce Rate Alert**
- **Cause**: Outdated email addresses, poor list quality
- **Action**: Verify email addresses, remove hard bounces, update contacts

**Benchmark Deviation Alert**
- **Cause**: Significant drop vs industry standards
- **Action**: Review campaign strategy, check deliverability, improve content

## Wellbeing Monitoring & Retention

### What is it?
Predict which candidates are likely to stay long-term and identify burnout risks before they become problems. This helps you hire for retention, not just skills.

### How to use it

#### For Employers: Wellbeing Dashboard

1. Navigate to **Wellbeing Dashboard**
2. Review organizational health metrics:
   - **Overall Health Score**: Company-wide wellbeing (0-100)
   - **Risk Distribution**: High/medium/low risk employees
   - **Department Breakdown**: Health by department
   - **Trends**: Historical burnout, engagement, retention
3. Review intervention recommendations
4. Implement suggested actions
5. Track improvements over time

#### For Hiring: Candidate Assessment

When reviewing candidates:
1. Look for the **Retention Score** on candidate profiles
2. Review key metrics:
   - **Burnout Risk Score**: Lower is better (0-100)
   - **Retention Probability**: Likelihood of staying 1 year
   - **Engagement Score**: Predicted engagement level
3. Check risk factors and protective factors
4. Review recommended interventions
5. Use insights to make better hiring decisions

#### Calculate Retention ROI

1. Go to **Retention ROI Calculator**
2. Review your metrics:
   - Total hires and retention rates
   - Cost analysis (recruitment, attrition, savings)
   - Quality metrics (performance scores, high performers)
3. Compare against industry benchmarks
4. Review recommendations for improvement
5. Track ROI over time

### Understanding Scores

**Burnout Risk Score (0-100)**
- **0-30**: Low risk - Excellent work-life balance
- **31-60**: Medium risk - Monitor for warning signs
- **61-100**: High risk - Proactive intervention needed

**Retention Probability (%)**
- **80-100%**: Excellent - Likely long-term employee
- **60-79%**: Good - Monitor and support
- **Below 60%**: Concerning - High attrition risk

**Engagement Score (0-100)**
- **80-100**: Highly engaged
- **60-79**: Moderately engaged
- **Below 60**: Disengaged

### Tips for Improving Retention
- Hire candidates with high retention scores
- Address burnout risks proactively
- Implement recommended interventions
- Monitor wellbeing trends regularly
- Focus on work-life balance and growth opportunities

## Strategic ROI Tracking

### What is it?
Track the long-term quality and performance of your hires to demonstrate the real value of Oracle Smart Recruitment.

### How to use it

#### Track Hire Quality
When you make a hire:
1. Go to **Strategic ROI**
2. Click **Track New Hire**
3. Enter hire details:
   - Candidate and job information
   - Hire date
   - Cost per hire
   - Time to hire (days)
4. Click **Save**

#### Update Performance Milestones
At key milestones (90 days, 180 days, 1 year):
1. Find the hire in your tracking dashboard
2. Click **Update Performance**
3. Enter milestone data:
   - Performance score (0-100)
   - Still employed? (Yes/No)
   - If terminated, reason
4. Click **Save**

#### View Success Stories
1. Navigate to **Success Stories**
2. Review top performing clients
3. See metrics like:
   - Hire count and quality scores
   - Retention rates
   - Cost and time savings
   - Before/after comparisons
4. Use stories for case studies and testimonials

#### Calculate Predictive ROI
For new clients or projects:
1. Go to **Predictive ROI Calculator**
2. Enter parameters:
   - Industry type
   - Company size
   - Projected number of hires
3. Review predictions:
   - Estimated cost per hire
   - Expected retention rate
   - Projected savings vs traditional
   - Confidence level
4. Use for proposals and business cases

#### Platform Value Dashboard
For overall performance:
1. View **Platform Value Dashboard**
2. Review aggregate metrics:
   - Total clients and hires
   - Average quality scores
   - Overall retention rates
   - Cost savings achieved
3. Compare client segments (Enterprise, Mid-Market, SMB)
4. Track trends over time

### ROI Metrics to Track
- **Cost per hire**: Target SAR 8,500 (vs SAR 18,000 traditional)

## B2B SaaS Tools

### What is it?
Collect employee engagement and organizational health data to improve AI matching and retention prediction.

### How to use it

#### Create Pulse Surveys
1. Go to **B2B Tools** > **Pulse Surveys**
2. Click **Create Survey**
3. Configure survey:
   - Survey name and type (satisfaction, engagement, wellbeing)
   - Questions (rating, text, multiple choice)
   - Target audience (all, department, role)
   - Frequency (one-time, weekly, monthly, quarterly)
   - Anonymous or identified
4. Click **Launch Survey**
5. Monitor responses in real-time

#### View Organizational Health
1. Navigate to **Organizational Health**
2. Select time period (month/year)
3. Review metrics:
   - Overall health score
   - Employee satisfaction
   - Engagement score
   - Turnover rate
   - Productivity score
4. Check department breakdown
5. Review trends (improving, declining, stable)
6. Implement recommendations

#### Perform Skill Gap Analysis
1. Go to **Skill Gap Analysis**
2. Select department (optional)
3. Review analysis:
   - Current skills inventory
   - Required skills for strategic goals
   - Identified gaps with impact
   - Training priorities (upskill existing)
   - Hiring priorities (recruit new talent)
4. Export report for planning

#### Predict Turnover Risk
1. Navigate to **Turnover Prediction**
2. Review organizational risk:
   - Overall risk score (0-100)
   - Estimated attrition (next 6 months)
   - High risk departments
   - Key risk factors
3. Implement prevention recommendations
4. Monitor risk trends over time

#### Get Labor Market Intelligence
1. Go to **Market Intelligence**
2. Enter region and industry
3. Review insights:
   - Average salary ranges
   - In-demand skills
   - Talent availability
   - Competition level
   - Hiring trends
4. Use for strategic planning

#### Submit Anonymous Feedback
For employees:
1. Click **Anonymous Feedback**
2. Select feedback type (suggestion, concern, praise, complaint)
3. Write feedback
4. Select category (optional)
5. Submit anonymously

### Best Practices
- Run pulse surveys quarterly for trends
- Act on feedback quickly to show responsiveness
- Use skill gap analysis for workforce planning
- Monitor turnover risk proactively
- Leverage market intelligence for competitive offers

## Analytics Export

### What is it?
Export your analytics data and reports in CSV or PDF format for external analysis, presentations, or record-keeping.

### How to use it

#### Export to CSV
For spreadsheet analysis:
1. Navigate to **Analytics**
2. Click **Export** button
3. Select **CSV format**
4. Choose date range
5. Click **Generate Export**
6. Download file when ready
7. Open in Excel or Google Sheets

#### Export to PDF
For presentations and reports:
1. Navigate to **Analytics**
2. Click **Export** button
3. Select **PDF format**
4. Choose date range
5. Click **Generate Export**
6. Download professional report when ready
7. Use in presentations or share with stakeholders

### What's Included in Exports

**CSV Exports**:
- Raw data for custom analysis
- All metrics and dimensions
- Timestamp information
- Ready for pivot tables and charts

**PDF Exports**:
- Professional formatted report
- Charts and visualizations
- Summary statistics
- Executive summary
- Date range and metadata

### Tips for Exporting
- Export monthly for trend analysis
- Use CSV for deep analysis in Excel
- Use PDF for stakeholder presentations
- Keep historical exports for year-over-year comparison
- Automate exports for regular reporting
